German is NOT an SVO language

omg, this is from GitHub!

Linguists classify English as a “SVO” Language

“SVO” describes the structure of a standard affirmative English sentence: Subject - Verb - Object.

Example:

The dog (subject) eats (verb) the apple (object).

German may also look like a SVO-language, if we read a sentence like 

“Der Hund isst den Apfel” = The dog eats the apple.

but this is a very specific case and therefore misleading.


Let’s give a look to other German sentences:

“Heute hat der Hund den Apfel gegessen” = Today the dog has eaten the apple
[Today - has - the dog - the apple - eaten] <= these translations in brackets respect the German sentence order

“Den Apfel hat heute der Hund gegessen” = Today the dog has eaten the apple
[the apple - has - today - the dog - eaten]


These sentences are correct and their structure is very common. Do you notice how their structure does not correspond to SVO anymore?

First thing: the verb “eaten” is at the end.

Second thing: in the first sentence, the verb “has” is before the subject.

Third thing: in the second sentence, the object (“apple”) comes before the verb, while the subject (the dog) comes later.

We have to conclude then, that German sentence structure is different from English, even though sometimes it looks identical.
